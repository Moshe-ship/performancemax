Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important factor in whether you show up in the Google Map Pack. Yet most local businesses set it up once and never touch it again.

That's a mistake. Google rewards active, complete, and optimized profiles. Here's how to get yours right.

## Why your GBP matters more than your website

For local searches, Google often shows the Map Pack *above* organic results. And the Map Pack is powered almost entirely by Google Business Profile data — not your website.

This means a business with a mediocre website but an excellent GBP will often outrank a business with a great website but a neglected profile. For local businesses, your GBP is your real homepage.

## The fundamentals: get these right first

### Business name
Use your real business name — exactly as it appears on your signage and legal documents. Don't stuff keywords into your business name. Google penalizes this, and it can get your profile suspended.

### Categories
Your primary category is the single most important ranking factor for your GBP. Choose the most specific category that accurately describes your business.

Then add relevant secondary categories. A dental office might use "Dentist" as primary, then add "Cosmetic Dentist," "Emergency Dental Service," and "Teeth Whitening Service" as secondary categories.

### Address and service area
If customers visit your location, use your physical address. If you serve customers at their location (like a plumber or electrician), set up a service area instead. You can do both.

### Hours
Keep your hours accurate — including holidays and special hours. Google tracks when customers search for you versus when you're listed as open. Mismatched hours hurt your ranking.

## Content that moves the needle

### Business description
You get 750 characters. Use them. Write a clear description of what you do, who you serve, and what areas you cover. Include your main services and service areas naturally — but don't keyword-stuff.

### Services and products
Add every service you offer with descriptions and prices where applicable. This gives Google more data to match you with relevant searches, and it helps potential customers understand what you offer before they click.

### Photos
Businesses with photos receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more click-throughs to their websites. Upload photos of your business, your team, your work, and your products. Add new photos regularly — Google treats this as a freshness signal.

### Google Posts
Post updates at least weekly. Share promotions, events, tips, or news. Each post gives Google fresh content to index and shows potential customers that your business is active.

## Reviews: the ranking factor you can influence

Reviews are the second most important ranking factor for the Map Pack, after your primary category. But it's not just about quantity — Google looks at:

- **Volume** — More reviews signal a more established business
- **Recency** — A steady flow of new reviews matters more than a burst of old ones
- **Rating** — Higher is obviously better, but a perfect 5.0 can actually look suspicious
- **Keywords in reviews** — When customers mention specific services in their reviews, it helps you rank for those terms
- **Response rate** — Responding to every review (positive and negative) signals that you're engaged

### How to get more reviews

The businesses that get the most reviews make it easy and ask consistently:

1. Create a direct review link from your GBP dashboard
2. Send the link via text or email after every job or visit
3. Train your team to ask for reviews as part of the closing process
4. Respond to every review within 24 hours

Don't offer incentives for reviews — Google's terms prohibit this, and it can get your reviews removed.

## Citations: consistency is everything

A citation is any online mention of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP). Google uses citations to verify your business information and assess your prominence.

The key is **consistency**. Your NAP should be identical everywhere — your website, GBP, Yelp, Facebook, industry directories, and every other listing. Even small differences (like "St." vs "Street") can confuse Google's algorithms.

Start with the major platforms: Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, Bing Places, and your industry-specific directories. Then expand to local directories, chamber of commerce listings, and anywhere else your business should be listed.

## Tracking what's working

Your GBP Insights dashboard shows you:
- How many people found you through search vs. maps
- What searches triggered your profile
- How many people called, visited your website, or requested directions
- Photo views compared to competitors

Review these monthly. They'll tell you whether your optimization efforts are working and where to focus next.

## The bottom line

Your Google Business Profile isn't a set-it-and-forget-it asset. It's a living marketing channel that needs regular attention. The businesses that treat it that way are the ones dominating the Map Pack in their area.
