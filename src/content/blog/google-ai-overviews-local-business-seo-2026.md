If you have searched for anything on Google in the last six months, you have noticed something different. Before the usual list of links, there is now a big AI-generated summary sitting at the top of the page. Google calls these AI Overviews, and they are fundamentally changing how people find local businesses.

This is not a small tweak. According to data from BrightLocal's 2026 Local Search Report, AI Overviews now appear in **47% of local intent searches**. That means nearly half the time someone searches for a service in their area, Google's AI is answering the question before they ever click on a website or a Map Pack listing.

For local business owners, this is either a threat or an opportunity. It depends entirely on whether you understand what is happening and adjust your strategy. Let's break it down.

## What AI Overviews Actually Are

AI Overviews are Google's way of using artificial intelligence to synthesize information from across the web and present a direct answer at the top of search results. Instead of giving you ten blue links and letting you figure out which one has the answer, Google now reads those pages for you and generates a summary.

For a search like "best way to fix a leaky faucet," the AI Overview pulls information from multiple plumbing websites and presents a step-by-step answer right on the results page.

For local searches, it works differently and this is the part that matters to you. When someone searches "best HVAC company near me" or "emergency plumber in Roswell GA," the AI Overview often includes a curated list of local businesses with brief descriptions of why each one was selected. It pulls from Google Business Profiles, review content, and website information to create these recommendations.

Think of it as Google's AI becoming a personal concierge that says, "Based on reviews, services offered, and proximity, here are the three businesses I recommend." That is very different from the old model where Google just showed you a list and let you decide.

## Why This Changes Everything for Local Businesses

The impact is significant and measurable. Here is what the data shows.

### Click-through rates are shifting

Searches with AI Overviews see a **23% reduction in clicks** to the traditional Map Pack and organic results, according to Semrush's 2026 search data. That traffic is not disappearing. It is being captured by the businesses that get featured in the AI Overview itself.

If your business shows up in the AI Overview, you are getting exposure that used to require a top-three Map Pack position or a first-page organic ranking. If you are not showing up, you are losing visibility even if your Map Pack ranking has not changed.

### The criteria are different

Here is the critical thing. The businesses that appear in AI Overviews are not always the same businesses that rank highest in the Map Pack. Google's AI weighs factors differently.

Review content matters more than review count alone. A business with 150 reviews that consistently mention specific services, quality, and responsiveness will often appear in AI Overviews over a business with 400 reviews that are mostly generic "great service" comments.

Website content depth matters significantly. Businesses with detailed service pages that answer common questions, explain their process, and include specific information about their service areas give the AI more material to work with.

Freshness signals carry more weight. Recent Google Business Profile posts, recent reviews, and recently updated website content all signal to the AI that this is an active, current business.

### Zero-click searches are growing

A portion of searchers now get what they need from the AI Overview without clicking anything. They see the AI's recommendation, note the business name and phone number, and call directly from the search results page. This means your traditional analytics might show fewer website visits even while your phone is ringing more.

This is why [conversion tracking](/blog/google-ads-mistakes-local-businesses) matters more than ever. If you are only measuring website clicks, you are missing the full picture.

## How to Get Your Business Featured in AI Overviews

Here is the practical part. These are the specific actions that increase your chances of appearing in Google's AI-generated results for local searches.

### 1. Level up your Google Business Profile content

We covered the basics of GBP optimization in our [complete guide](/blog/google-business-profile-optimization-guide), but AI Overviews raise the bar. Filling out every field is no longer enough. You need to make every field work harder.

Your business description should read like a pitch to a knowledgeable friend. Not keyword-stuffed, not generic. Specific details about what makes your business different, what services you specialize in, and what areas you serve.

Your service listings need real descriptions, not just names. Instead of "AC Repair," write "Same-day AC repair for residential and commercial systems including central air, ductless mini-splits, and heat pumps. Available 24/7 for emergencies in Roswell, Alpharetta, and surrounding areas." That gives the AI specifics to pull from.

Your Google Posts should demonstrate expertise, not just announce promotions. Share a tip about seasonal maintenance. Explain a common problem and how you solve it. Post a brief case study of a recent job (without identifying details, of course). This content feeds the AI's understanding of your business.

### 2. Build review content that the AI can use

Reviews have always mattered for [local SEO](/blog/local-seo-vs-organic-seo-what-local-businesses-need-to-know), but AI Overviews use review content in a new way. The AI reads and synthesizes your reviews to generate descriptions of your business. That means the words your customers use in their reviews directly shape how the AI describes you to potential customers.

You cannot control what people write (and you should never try to script reviews). But you can influence it by delivering specific, memorable experiences and by making it easy for customers to mention those details.

When you ask for a review, try being specific: "Would you mind leaving us a review? It really helps if you mention what service we did and how the experience was." This is not review manipulation. It is simply asking for useful detail instead of a generic star rating.

Here is what review content the AI tends to highlight:

- **Specific services mentioned by name.** "They replaced our water heater in under three hours" gives the AI more to work with than "good plumber."
- **Response time and reliability.** "They showed up within 45 minutes of my call" is exactly the kind of detail the AI surfaces.
- **Unique qualities.** "They cleaned up everything perfectly when they finished" differentiates you from competitors.
- **Area mentions.** "Best electrician in Roswell" gives the AI a geographic relevance signal.

For more on building a review system that generates this kind of detailed feedback, see our [review generation guide](/blog/how-to-get-more-online-reviews-for-your-local-business).

### 3. Create content that answers questions comprehensively

AI Overviews pull information from websites that provide thorough, direct answers to questions. For local businesses, this means your website content strategy needs to shift.

Stop thinking about keywords. Start thinking about questions.

What does a potential customer ask before they hire someone like you? What concerns do they have? What do they not understand about your service? Each of those questions is a content opportunity.

For example, a roofing company might create pages that answer:

- "How much does a roof replacement cost in Georgia in 2026?"
- "How do I know if my roof needs repair or full replacement?"
- "What should I look for when hiring a roofing contractor?"
- "How long does a roof replacement take?"
- "Does homeowners insurance cover roof damage from storms?"

Each page should be 600 to 1,000 words of genuinely useful information. Include specific numbers where possible (average costs in your area, typical timelines, warranty details). The AI loves specifics. Vague, generic content gets ignored.

Structure your content with clear headings and subheadings. Use question-based headers that match how people actually search. The AI uses these structural cues to extract relevant answers.

### 4. Add structured data to your website

Structured data (also called schema markup) is code that helps Google understand what your content is about. It has always helped with SEO, but it is even more important for AI Overviews because it makes it easier for the AI to parse and use your information.

The most important schema types for local businesses:

- **LocalBusiness schema** with your business name, address, phone number, hours, and service areas
- **Service schema** for each service you offer, with descriptions and pricing if available
- **FAQPage schema** for any FAQ content on your site
- **Review schema** that showcases your aggregate rating

If your website already has structured data (check by running your URL through Google's Rich Results Test), make sure it is complete and accurate. If it does not have structured data, this should be a priority. It is one of the highest-impact technical changes you can make for AI search visibility.

### 5. Keep everything fresh

The AI favors recent, active signals. This means:

- **Post on your Google Business Profile weekly.** We have been saying this for years, but with AI Overviews it matters even more. Fresh posts tell the AI your business is active and current.
- **Update your website content quarterly.** Review your service pages and make sure pricing, services, and details are current. Add new information as your business evolves.
- **Respond to reviews immediately.** Not just because it helps with rankings, but because AI Overviews factor in engagement signals. A business that responds to every review within hours looks more engaged than one that responds sporadically.
- **Add new photos to your GBP monthly.** Fresh visual content is another activity signal that the AI picks up on.

## What This Means for Google Ads

If you are running [Google Ads](/services), AI Overviews create both a challenge and an opportunity.

The challenge: AI Overviews push paid ads slightly further down the page for some searches, which can reduce visibility for certain ad positions.

The opportunity: Google has started integrating sponsored results into AI Overviews for local searches. Early data shows these placements have click-through rates **2.3x higher** than traditional search ads because they appear within the AI's recommendation context rather than as a separate ad block.

Performance Max campaigns are particularly well-positioned for this because they already run across all Google surfaces, including these new AI-integrated placements. If you are running PMax (and if you are not sure what that is, read our [Performance Max guide](/blog/what-is-performance-max-campaign-google-ads)), your ads are automatically eligible for these AI Overview placements.

The key to getting your ads featured in AI Overview placements is the same as getting your organic presence featured: detailed, relevant business information. Make sure your Google Business Profile is linked to your ad account, your landing pages are content-rich, and your ad assets include specific, detailed descriptions of your services.

## The Businesses That Will Win

The shift to AI-powered search rewards the same qualities that have always separated great local businesses from average ones: specificity, consistency, and genuine expertise.

Businesses that provide detailed, specific information about their services will appear in AI Overviews more often than businesses with thin, generic content. Businesses that maintain a consistent presence through regular posts, fresh reviews, and updated content will be favored over businesses that set up their profile once and forgot about it. Businesses that demonstrate genuine expertise through helpful content and detailed customer interactions will earn the AI's trust more than businesses that just chase keywords.

AI Overviews do not invalidate any of that work. They reward it even more.

## What Not to Panic About

Some business owners hear "AI is changing search" and think the sky is falling. It is not. Here is what you do not need to worry about.

**The Map Pack is not going away.** Google still shows the Map Pack for local searches, and it still gets strong engagement. AI Overviews sit above or alongside the Map Pack, but they do not replace it.

**Your existing SEO work is not wasted.** Everything that helps you rank organically also helps you appear in AI Overviews. Good content, strong reviews, complete business profiles. None of that changes.

**You do not need to hire an "AI SEO" specialist.** Ignore anyone selling "AI Optimization" as a separate service. The fundamentals are the same. The bar is just higher for content quality and completeness.

**Small businesses are not at a disadvantage.** If anything, AI Overviews can level the playing field. A small business with 80 detailed, recent reviews can outperform a large chain with 2,000 generic reviews in AI Overviews because the AI values content quality over raw volume.

## Your Action Plan for This Month

Do not try to do everything at once. Here is what to prioritize right now:

1. **Audit your Google Business Profile.** Is every field complete? Are your service descriptions detailed and specific? When was your last post? Update anything that is outdated or generic.
2. **Review your last 20 customer reviews.** Do they mention specific services by name? Do they include details about the experience? If most reviews are just "great service, 5 stars," adjust how you ask for reviews.
3. **Check your website service pages.** Does each major service have its own page with at least 600 words of useful, specific content? If not, start with your highest-revenue service and build from there.
4. **Verify your structured data.** Run your homepage and key service pages through Google's Rich Results Test. If you do not have LocalBusiness and Service schema, add it.
5. **Set up a content calendar.** One Google Business Profile post per week. One website content update per month. One review response session per day (or at least every other day).

This is not a massive overhaul. It is a series of incremental improvements that compound over time. The businesses that start adapting now will be the ones dominating AI search results six months from now.
