If you have been running Google Ads for your local business or thinking about starting, you have probably heard the term Performance Max thrown around. Google has been pushing this campaign type aggressively since 2022, and by 2026 it has become the default recommendation for many advertisers.

But here is the problem. Most of the information out there about Performance Max is written for e-commerce brands and large advertisers. Local business owners are left wondering whether this campaign type even makes sense for a plumber in Roswell or a dentist in Shaker Heights.

We have managed Performance Max campaigns across hundreds of local business accounts, and the answer is nuanced. It can be incredibly powerful for local businesses, but only when set up correctly. Done wrong, it will burn through your budget faster than any other campaign type.

## What Performance Max actually is

Performance Max, often shortened to PMax, is a goal-based campaign type that runs your ads across every Google advertising channel simultaneously. That means Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Google Maps, and the Discover feed, all from a single campaign.

Traditional Google Ads campaigns make you choose one channel at a time. You set up a Search campaign for text ads, a separate Display campaign for banner ads, a separate YouTube campaign for video. Performance Max eliminates that separation. You give Google your creative assets, your budget, and your conversion goals, and Google's AI decides where to show your ads, when to show them, and to whom.

The AI uses machine learning to optimize across all channels in real time, shifting budget automatically to wherever it is getting the best results. If your Search ads are converting well on Tuesday mornings but YouTube is performing better on Thursday evenings, PMax adjusts without you having to touch anything.

## How it works for local businesses

For local businesses, Performance Max has a specific advantage that most guides overlook. When you link your Google Business Profile to your PMax campaign, it unlocks local ad formats. Your business can appear in Google Maps search results, in the local finder, and in local ad placements that are not available through standard Search campaigns.

This means a single PMax campaign can simultaneously put your business at the top of Google Search when someone types "plumber near me," show your ad in Google Maps when someone is browsing nearby plumbers, display your banner ad on relevant websites to people in your service area, and show a short video ad on YouTube to local viewers who match your customer profile.

That is a level of coverage that would require four or five separate campaigns to achieve with traditional campaign types. And the AI optimization means your budget flows to whichever channel is generating the most leads at any given moment.

## The setup that works for local businesses

In our post about [common Google Ads mistakes](/blog/google-ads-mistakes-local-businesses), we covered the fundamentals like proper location targeting and conversion tracking. Those basics are even more critical with Performance Max because the AI needs accurate signals to optimize effectively.

Here is the setup framework we use across our local business accounts.

### Conversion tracking is absolutely non-negotiable

PMax relies entirely on conversion data to optimize. If you do not have proper tracking for phone calls, form submissions, and direction requests, the AI has no signal to optimize toward. It will just spend your money across channels with no idea what a good result looks like.

Set up conversion tracking before you launch a PMax campaign. Not after. Not "when you get around to it." Before. This is the single biggest factor in whether your campaign succeeds or fails. We covered this in depth in our [Google Ads mistakes guide](/blog/google-ads-mistakes-local-businesses).

### Asset groups should match your services

PMax uses asset groups instead of traditional ad groups. Each asset group contains headlines, descriptions, images, and optionally videos. For local businesses, create one asset group per major service category.

A home services company might have one asset group for plumbing, one for HVAC, and one for electrical. Each group gets its own headlines, descriptions, and images tailored to that specific service. This gives Google the right creative assets to show the right message to the right person.

### Audience signals are suggestions, not targets

PMax lets you provide audience signals, which are hints about who your ideal customer is. You can upload customer lists, define custom segments based on search behavior, or use Google's pre-built audience categories.

The key word is signals. Unlike traditional campaigns where you set hard targeting rules, PMax uses your signals as a starting point and then expands beyond them if the AI finds better-performing audiences. Think of it as telling the AI where to start looking, not where to stop looking.

For local businesses, the most effective audience signals are your existing customer list if you have email addresses, custom segments based on searches like "plumber near me" or "best dentist in [city]," and in-market audiences for your specific service category.

### Location targeting still matters

Even though PMax uses AI for most decisions, you still control geographic targeting. Set your campaign to target only the areas you actually serve. Use radius targeting around your business location and exclude areas where you do not operate.

This is especially important for PMax because the AI's tendency to expand beyond your initial parameters applies to geography too. If you leave the default "Presence or interest" targeting, you will get clicks from people across the country who are interested in your area but will never become customers.

## When Performance Max does not make sense

PMax is not the right choice for every local business in every situation.

If you have a very small budget under $500 per month, a focused Search campaign targeting your highest-intent keywords will likely perform better. PMax needs enough data to optimize, and very small budgets do not generate enough conversions for the AI to learn effectively.

If you are in a niche with very specific high-intent keywords, a traditional Search campaign gives you more control over exactly which searches trigger your ads. PMax might spread your budget across channels where your customers are not looking.

If you do not have conversion tracking set up, do not launch PMax. Full stop. Get tracking in place first, ideally run a standard Search campaign for a few weeks to generate baseline conversion data, and then launch PMax with that foundation.

## How to know if your PMax campaign is working

The metrics that matter for local businesses are cost per lead, lead volume, and lead quality. Not impressions, not clicks, not click-through rate.

Check your cost per lead weekly. Compare it to what a customer is worth to your business. If your average customer is worth $500 and your cost per lead is $30 with a 20% close rate, your cost per acquired customer is $150. That is a 3.3x return, which is excellent for most local businesses.

If your cost per lead is climbing or your lead quality is declining, it usually means one of three things: your creative assets need refreshing, your conversion tracking has broken, or your budget is too low for the AI to optimize effectively.
